The Problem

Research moves at the speed of context.

Modern R&D is splintered across a dozen tools that don't talk to each other. The cost is paid in lost time, lost insight, and lost momentum.

▦

Knowledge silos

Project context lives in fragments: a Slack thread, a notebook, a stale README, a PI's head. New collaborators re-learn it from scratch.

⚙

Tool fragmentation

You stitch together GitHub, Notion, Jupyter, ChatGPT, Zotero. Every handoff loses something. You are the integration layer.

◷

Lost context

Generic AI assistants don't know your model, your data, or last week's lab meeting. They produce confident answers about the wrong problem.

The Product

An interface that learns alongside you.

Tuva indexes your project end-to-end, then puts that context to work — surfacing, connecting, and automating across the tools you already use.

01 · Knowledge

Continuous Context Mapping

Tuva builds a rich knowledge graph of your research as it evolves. Different pieces of context — across papers, experiments, datasets — light up as they relate to your current work. Discover connections you'd miss in a spreadsheet.

  • Continuous re-indexing of source files and PDFs
  • Citations down to the line, page, or notebook cell
  • Surfaces relationships across papers, experiments, and datasets
02 · Interoperability

Interoperability

Connect to the tools your team already loves. Tuva integrates with your existing research stack — so context flows between them instead of stalling at every boundary.

  • Native integrations with GitHub, Notion, Slack, and Jupyter
  • Local-first by default — your data stays where it lives
  • No vendor lock-in: take your knowledge graph with you
03 · Automation

Skill-Building

Design repeatable actions and codify them as skills. Let Tuva automate the workflows you build — figure regeneration, lit-review summaries, parameter sweeps — into repeatable skills your whole team can call on.

  • Turn ad-hoc prompts into versioned, shareable skills
  • Compose skills into multi-step research pipelines
  • Run on-demand or schedule against new data

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Don't see yours? Drop us a line.

At Tuva, we are not attempting to automate away the scientific process. Instead, we are building an intelligent workspace for your research. It is a workspace that understands the context and direction of your project from start to finish; that will connect you to specialized tools while maintaining consistency between them; and that will allow you to collaborate with a tool that already understands your research plan and workflows. Tuva indexes your whole project context – code, notebooks, PDFs, meeting notes, planning documents – into one intelligent and dynamic knowledge base, and pulls the right information in at the right time. It is built so that you can keep using the same stack of tools you do now, but with more consistency in results. Jumping between open tools means you are the integration layer. Tuva closes that gap.
The only data that leaves your machine is the metadata around your tool usage - i.e. whether and how much you are using Tuva - and the information necessary for user authentication. None of the contents of your research, nor of your interactions with Tuva, are shared with us or the developers of the models on which Tuva runs. That being said, researchers working in regulated environments (HIPAA, ITAR, IRB-restricted data, industry NDAs) should consult with their PIs and management before using Tuva to ensure compliance.
Tuva currently works particularly well for source code, Jupyter notebooks, markdown notes, PDFs, and data README-style files. If a specific format is load-bearing for your workflow, ask before you download. We would rather tell you "not yet" than have you find out after install.
Tuva automatically re-indexes as files change. You can always review the current state of the knowledge base, and sources can be added or removed explicitly. Answers will cite into the exact location, matching the up-to-date state: page 3 of the paper, line 86 of model.py, a function name, a notebook cell. When you edit a database, Tuva will point to the new line numbers. When you drop a new PDF into the folder, it joins the corpus without a manual rebuild.
